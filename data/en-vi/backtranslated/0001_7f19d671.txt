SOME DIFFERENCES IN THE CALCULATION OF LOADS AND ACTIONS BETWEEN TCVN 2737:2020 AND TCVN 2737:1995, APPLIED TO CONSTRUCTION WORKS
NGUYEN NGOC PHUC
Faculty of Construction Engineering, Industrial University of Ho Chi Minh City
phd.nguyenngocphuc@gmail.com
Abstract.
Accurately determining both the values and all components of loads and actions is a very important factor in structural design calculations.
This factor determines the economic and technical requirements of a project, as reflected in the total construction investment and constraints on durability, stability, deformation, and service life of the structure.
In practice, many latent and emerging issues related to construction works are caused by inadequate assessment of loads and actions.
Moreover, loads and actions do not possess inherent values or modes of action; rather, they depend on changes in natural conditions and socio-economic factors.
Nowadays, we are facing significant changes in loadsвАФnot only unusual modes of action due to socio-economic conditions, but also dramatic changes arising from natural conditions.
At the same time, as our country advances in the process of international integration and globalization, systematically adopting technical standards is inevitable.
In this article, I discuss some differences in the calculation of loads and actions between TCVN 2737:2020 and TCVN 2737:1995, as applied to civil and industrial construction works.
Keywords: TCVN 2737:1995; TCVN 2737:2020; Loads and actions.
THE DIFFERENTIATIONS FOR DETERMINATION OF ACTIONS ON STRUCTURES BETWEEN TCVN 2737:2020 AND TCVN 2737:1995, USED IN CIVIL ENGINEERING
Abstract.
In this paper, I focus on the differences in the determination of actions on structures between TCVN 2737:2020 and TCVN 2737:1995, as applied in Civil Engineering.
As we know, actions and loads are not constant due to changes in natural conditions and social development.
On the other hand, our country is currently in the process of internationalization and globalization.
Therefore, we must align our standards with those of the world.
Determining the exact values and the way actions affect structures is of utmost importance.
They influence the safety, durability, and cost of construction projects.
Keywords: TCVN 2737:1995; TCVN 2737:2020; Actions on structures

COMBINATION OF ACTIONS:
2.1 Load Combinations in TCVN 2737:1995 [1]
This standard proposes to control the structural serviceability in two limit states with two types of load combination structures.
The concept of load combination mentioned in this standard can easily lead to misunderstandings, as it is often interpreted as a combination of causes rather than an accurate understanding of the combination of effects of actions.
This is because most effects of actions are resolved based on the principle of independent action, which allows for the acceptance of small deformations and displacements in the structure; thus, the combination of effects of actions by linear addition is similar to the linear addition of the causes of actions.

2.2 Combination of internal forces and displacements in TCVN 2737:2020 [2]
This standard still proposes controlling the structural performance by two limit states and adds control of the allowable stress state.
The combination structures are described in more detail and greater emphasis is placed on the consequences of actions.
The reason for addressing combinations based on the consequences of actions is due to the material nonlinearity and geometric nonlinearity of structures under loads.
That is, it is not possible to simply accept the principle of independent action in a purely straightforward manner.
Therefore, this method of combination allows for a more convenient approach to technical standards and more accurately reflects the structural behavior.

3.
VALUES OF DEAD LOAD, LIVE LOAD, AND BASIC CHARACTERISTIC PARAMETERS: [1],[2],[3]
The TCVN 2737:2020 standard provides detailed regulations on the characteristic parameters used to determine dead load values, including the volumetric weight parameters of various types of materials.
For live load, the TCVN 2737:2020 standard only specifies the total value and does not specify the value for the long-term component.
In addition, there are adjustments and additions to live loads for certain functions:
- The live load for stands is separated and not generally specified within the group of loads for meeting rooms, ballrooms, etc.
- Additional live loads are included for commercial areas, exhibitions, and display spaces.
- Two new live load values have been added to the group for workshop functions.
- Live load for refuge areas in high-rise buildings has been added.
- Live loads have been supplemented for floors in special areas.
- Additional live loads have been included for technical floors.
- The live load value in the mezzanine function group has been adjusted and supplemented with one additional value.
- A specific value is provided for the livestock barn function group instead of specifying a lower limit value.
- The value has been modified and one live load value added to the flat roofs in use function group.
- A lower limit value is specified for live loads in the platform function group.
- Additional live loads have been specified for the function group of waiting rooms and passenger check-in and waiting areas at airports.
- Live loads for the car garage function group have been adjusted and supplemented.
- Live load values have been added for the fire engine function group.
- Live load values have been specified for the helicopter landing area function group.
Compared to TCVN 2737:1995, TCVN 2737:2020 has eliminated the load reduction factor пБєA for areas subjected to loads greater than 9m2 and only allows the use of the load reduction factor пБєA for areas subjected to loads greater than 36m2.
In the TCVN 2737:1995 standard, the load combination reliability factor for crane loads is taken as 1.1.
In TCVN 2737:2020, the adjusted value for ќ≥Q is 1.5; the recommended combination factors are вИСѕЖ0i = 0.7 and вИСѕЖ2i = 0.3.
The auxiliary coefficients used to calculate the strength of crane beams subjected to vertical concentrated loads caused by crane wheels have also been adjusted; these coefficients are lower than those in TCVN 2737:1995.
In TCVN 2737:2020, the requirements for calculating structures subjected to crane loads are specified within the scope of the design task and are not detailed as in TCVN 2737:1995.

The structural analysis is no longer solved under the influence of two components, namely static wind and dynamic wind; instead, the Gust factor (Gf) is used.
The wind load also takes into account the importance factor of the structure (Iw).

CONCLUSION:
- The TCVN 2737:2020 standard provides more detailed specifications regarding the structure of combination effects, replacing the simpler load combination concept in TCVN 2737:1995.
- The TCVN 2737:2020 standard specifies more detailed characteristic parameters for calculating dead load values, including the volumetric weight of different material types.
- The TCVN 2737:2020 standard adjusts and supplements live load values according to usage functions, reflecting the current, more diverse practices compared to TCVN 2737:1995.
- The TCVN 2737:2020 standard takes a more open approach to the calculation of wind loads on structures; wind pressure zoning and intensity identification have also undergone more changes compared to TCVN 2737:1995.
