Abstract
Advancing towards socialism remains the historical choice of Vietnam in the current period.
Summarizing 35 years of renewal, the 13th National Party Congress clearly stated that the conception of socialism and the path towards socialism in our country are increasingly being refined and gradually realized.
Socialism remains the future of human society; it continues to represent the will and aspiration of the Vietnamese people; and the Communist Party of Vietnam remains the sole force leading the cause of building socialism in Vietnam.
In this article, the author analyzes and clarifies socialism and the path toward socialism in Vietnam according to the perspectives of the 13th National Party Congress.

Content 2.1.
On Socialism According to the Viewpoint of the 13th National Party Congress
President Ho Chi Minh always remained faithful to Marxism-Leninism, but with his inherent spirit of independence and freedom, he profoundly and creatively absorbed the essence and objectives of socialism.
He once asked the question: “What is socialism?” and then answered it himself, clearly defining the most fundamental characteristics of socialism, reflecting the conditions of Vietnam as well as the needs and aspirations of the Vietnamese people.
The great contribution of C. Marx and F. Engels was the discovery of the inevitable trend in the development of human society, leading to the conclusion that capitalism would inevitably be replaced by communism, with socialism as its initial stage.
In the Manifesto of the Communist Party, Marx and Engels foresaw a future society: “In place of the old bourgeois society, with its classes and class antagonisms, we shall have an association in which the free development of each is the condition for the free development of all.” (Collected Works of Marx and Engels, 1995, vol.
4, p. 628).
The superiority of socialism and communism lies in the abolition of all forms of exploitation, oppression, and injustice caused by capitalist relations of production.
Later, in the work Socialism: Utopian and Scientific, Engels also put forward a thesis: “To make socialism a science, it must first be placed on a real basis.” (Collected Works of Marx and Engels, 1995, vol.
19, p. 293).
“Socialism means making the people prosperous and the country strong…” (Ho Chi Minh Collected Works, 2011, vol.
8, p. 226); “Socialism is about justice and equity; one who does not work shall not eat.
The elderly or disabled will be assisted and cared for by the State.” (Ho Chi Minh, vol.
9, p. 175); “Socialism firstly frees labor from destitution, ensures jobs for all, so that everyone is warm, well-fed, and leads a happy life.” (Ho Chi Minh Collected Works, 2011, vol.
10, p. 17).

Lenin, based on the views of C. Marx and F. Engels on communism, clearly indicated the unity of purpose between socialism and communism: “…when we begin to implement socialist reforms, we must make clear the ultimate goal of these reforms, namely, the establishment of a communist society—a society that is not merely limited to the expropriation of factories, plants, land, and means of production, nor merely limited to the strict inventory and control of the production and distribution of products, but goes even further, towards the realization of the principle: from each according to his ability, to each according to his needs.
Therefore, the name ‘communist party’ is the only one that is scientifically accurate” (V. I. Lenin Collected Works, 1976, vol.
36, p. 56).
National independence in association with socialism is the red thread running throughout, the central and core feature of the entire ideological legacy of Ho Chi Minh.
It is also the primary and consistent feature of the revolutionary line of the Party from its inception to the present.
The standpoint of our Party on socialism, as well as the entire revolutionary line for socialist reforms, is firmly based on this principle.
Theoretical thought on socialism only began to emerge and take shape in our country after the founding of the Party, as embodied in the Brief Political Platform and Brief Strategy drafted and presented by Nguyen Ai Quoc at the founding Conference of the Party in early 1930.
It was also mentioned in the Political Thesis (October 1930) of the Party.
At that time, socialism was defined as an inevitable trend of the Vietnamese revolution, the chosen mode of development for Vietnamese society.
“To save the country and liberate the nation, there is no other path than the proletarian revolution” (Ho Chi Minh Collected Works, 2011, vol.
9, p. 314).
National independence must be linked to socialism and “…only socialism, only communism, can liberate oppressed nations and workers worldwide from slavery” (Ho Chi Minh Collected Works, 2011, vol.
9, p. 314).
From its very inception and throughout the revolutionary struggle, the Communist Party of Vietnam has always affirmed that socialism is the goal and ideal of the Communist Party and the Vietnamese people; advancing towards socialism is an objective demand, the inevitable path of the Vietnamese revolution.
This is clearly demonstrated in the particular path to socialism in Vietnam, where, starting from a semi-feudal colonial country, after winning independence, it bypassed the development of the capitalist system and marched directly towards socialism.

The new-type bourgeois democratic revolution (that is, the national democratic people’s revolution) led by the Party overthrew imperialist colonialism, gained independence and freedom, and resolved the land issue for the peasants (through an agrarian revolution to abolish the feudal system); then, bypassing the “capitalist stage”, thanks to international support, “struggled straight toward the path of socialism.” The transition to socialism, bypassing the capitalist system, is the only correct and scientific choice, reflecting the objective development laws of the Vietnamese revolution.
The Party’s 1930 Political Platform stated clearly that, after completing the national and people’s democratic revolution, the next step would be to advance to socialism.
This was a decisive and correct choice by the Party, meeting the earnest aspirations of the nation and the people, reflecting the prevailing trend of the era, and in alignment with the scientific, revolutionary, and creative viewpoints of Marxism-Leninism.

The purpose of economic development is for the people, to liberate the productive forces, and to develop the economy in order to improve the quality of life for everyone, so that all people can enjoy the fruits of development.
Here, the difference lies in the aim of serving all for profit in the interests of capitalists, protecting and developing capitalism.
Developing the economy with various forms of ownership (public, collective, and private ownership) and multiple economic sectors (the state sector, the collective sector, the private sector, the state-capitalist sector, and the sector with foreign-invested capital) aims to unlock the full potential of all economic sectors, individuals, and regions.
In a multi-sectoral economy, the state sector plays the leading role and serves as the primary instrument for the State to regulate the economy and orient its development.
In terms of politics: Socialism has a democratic political system.
The socialist state is a state of the people, by the people, and for the people, based on the great national unity bloc, with the core being the alliance of workers, peasants, and intellectuals under the leadership of the Communist Party.
"In all the work of the Party and the State, it is necessary to deeply grasp the view that 'the people are the root'; to truly trust, respect, and promote the people’s right to mastery; to persistently implement the motto 'the people know, the people discuss, the people do, the people inspect, the people supervise, the people enjoy the benefits'; to take the happiness and prosperity of the people as the goal to strive for.
To tighten the close relationship between the Party and the people; to strengthen and enhance the people’s trust in the Party, the State, and the socialist system" (Communist Party of Vietnam, 2021, vol.

After the Spring Victory of 1975, the country achieved reunification and entered the transitional period toward socialism, focusing on building and defending the Fatherland.
The transition from a wartime environment to one of peace and reconstruction—from the task of national independence to those of building and organizing a new way of life, developing the economy and culture, and constantly improving the living standards of the people—meant that the realities of people’s lives and the demands of national development led to new perceptions about socialism and the path toward building socialism.
The new circumstances brought forth new requirements, necessitating comprehensive and synchronous renewal on the part of our Party, State, and people in every aspect: from organizational operations and apparatus to individuals; from awareness to action; from leadership methods and approaches to implementation mechanisms and policies—at all levels, in every sector, and above all, within the Communist Party of Vietnam.
First and foremost, the Party had to renew its thinking about socialism and socialist construction.
Throughout the years of implementing Doi Moi, by drawing on practical experience and theoretical research, the Communist Party of Vietnam gradually acquired increasingly accurate and profound understanding of socialism and the transitional period toward socialism in Vietnam, which is a society characterized by:
Social orientation and distribution: Advancing progress and social justice at every stage and in every development policy; closely and synchronously linking economic growth with the development of society, culture, education, and training, and effectively resolving social issues for the goal of human development.
“[It is necessary to] fully understand and ensure the socialist orientation in social policies” (Communist Party of Vietnam, 2021, vol.
1, p. 147).
In the realm of distribution, this is reflected in a distribution system primarily based on labor outcomes, economic efficiency, as well as the system of social welfare and social security.
At the same time, to mobilize all economic resources for development, distribution according to capital contributions and other resources is also implemented.
Economy: It is a socialist-oriented market economy—a modern, internationally integrated market economy that operates fully and synchronously according to the laws of the market economy, under the management of the socialist law-governed state and under the leadership of the Communist Party of Vietnam; ensuring a socialist orientation with the goal of “a prosperous people, a strong country, a democratic, just, and civilized society,” with a high sense of responsibility in addressing global issues in a spirit of respect, peace, cooperation, and development.
The 13th National Party Congress was a great success in many aspects, thanks in part to the interest, contributions, and support of the whole society.
Especially in 2020, when the Covid-19 pandemic severely impacted our country, causing significant socio-economic damage and affecting national development, it was the strength of great national unity, the superiority of the socialist system, and the resolute, coordinated participation of the entire political system, under the correct leadership of the Party and with the united support of the people, that enabled us to gradually succeed in controlling the Covid-19 pandemic.
Socialism features healthy, just, and equitable social relationships, where people enjoy liberation and comprehensive development.
Those who contribute more are entitled to more, those who contribute less receive less, and those who do not work do not benefit, while the elderly and disabled receive help and care from the State.

2.2.
On the Pathway to Socialism in Vietnam According to the Perspective of the 13th National Party Congress
In his article “Some Theoretical and Practical Issues about Socialism and the Pathway to Socialism in Vietnam,” General Secretary Nguyen Phu Trong clarified the core values and distinctive features of socialism in Vietnam: “We need a society in which development genuinely serves humanity, rather than pursuing profit by exploiting and trampling on human dignity”; “Strive to build a civilized and healthy society for the legitimate interests and dignity of human beings, with ever-higher levels of knowledge, ethics, physical strength, lifestyle, and aesthetics.
We affirm: People hold the central position in the development strategy... A socialist society is one that aims at progressive and humane values, based on the common interests of the entire society in harmony with the legitimate interests of individuals, fundamentally different from societies in which individuals and groups compete to seize private gain at others’ expense” (Nguyen, 2021, p. 2).
Deeply imbued with Marxism-Leninism’s thinking about the pathway to socialism, our Party has creatively applied these principles to Vietnam’s specific conditions.
The Party has chosen for our country the path of transitioning to socialism, bypassing the capitalist system.
“This is a very difficult and complex undertaking and, therefore, inevitably requires a long period of transition with many stages and various forms of transitional socio-economic organization” (Communist Party of Vietnam, 2011, p. 67).
This path not only aligns with the necessities of history, with the laws and development trends of the era, but above all, it is suited to the current concrete historical context of our country.
As for the driving force: socialism belongs to the masses of the people and is built by the people themselves.
The success or failure of a regime depends on whether the people support and defend it.
To achieve this, it is essential to continually nurture and develop all aspects of this great unity bloc of the people, with the core being the alliance of workers, peasants, and intellectuals.
This is the social foundation of the Party and the State, and it is the decisive force determining the stability or collapse of the regime.
In the 10-year Socio-Economic Development Strategy (2021-2030), our Party emphasizes its perspective on human resources: “To maximize the human factor, considering people as the center, the subject, the most important resource, and the goal of development… all policies of the Party and State must aim to improve the material and spiritual life and happiness of the people” (Communist Party of Vietnam, 2021, Vol.
1, pp.
215-216).
In the current period, the will and aspirations of the Vietnamese people remain the pursuit of national independence associated with socialism; firmly safeguarding the independence and unification of the Fatherland, building and developing a powerful, prosperous, and happy nation, standing shoulder to shoulder with other countries in the world, and holding a worthy position on the world map.
In the late twentieth century, although actually existing socialism suffered a major collapse globally, the system of socialist countries ceased to exist, and the socialist movement entered a period of crisis and decline, facing tremendous difficulties, the Communist Party of Vietnam continued to affirm its commitment: our Party and people are determined to build Vietnam along the path to socialism on the basis of Marxism-Leninism and Ho Chi Minh Thought.
By the 11th National Congress, on the basis of reviewing 25 years of renewal, our Party’s understanding of socialism and the pathway to socialism had made new progress.
In the Platform for National Construction in the Period of Transition to Socialism (supplemented and developed in 2011), our Party once again affirmed: “Advancing toward socialism is the aspiration of our people, the correct choice of the Communist Party of Vietnam and President Ho Chi Minh, and in accordance with the developmental trends of history” (Communist Party of Vietnam, 2011, p. ...).

The eight characteristics outlined above comprehensively and thoroughly reflect the essential features of socialism that our Party and people are building.
Among them, the characteristic “prosperous people, a strong country, democracy, justice, and civilization” is both a feature and the goal of socialism in Vietnam.
This represents the general model of socialism in Vietnam.
The 13th National Party Congress was held at a time when the entire Party, people, and military had successfully implemented many policies, goals, and tasks set out in the Resolution of the 12th National Party Congress; contributing to accentuating the tremendous achievements of 35 years of renovation; 30 years of implementing the 1991 Platform; 10 years of executing the supplemented and developed 2011 Platform and the 2011–2020 Socio-Economic Development Strategy (Communist Party of Vietnam, 2021, Vol.

The 13th Congress demonstrated the mettle, indomitable will, and unwavering resolve of the entire nation towards the goal of prosperous people, a strong country, democracy, justice, and civilization.
At the same time, to successfully achieve these goals, the entire Party and people must elevate their revolutionary offensive spirit, self-reliance, and resilience, unleash all potentials and intellect, seize opportunities, overcome challenges.
The Platform for National Construction in the Period of Transition to Socialism (supplemented and developed in 2011) has set out eight major directions for building socialism: “First, vigorously promote industrialization and modernization associated with the development of a knowledge-based economy and environmental protection; second, develop a socialist-oriented market economy; third, build an advanced culture imbued with national identity; develop people, improve the living standards of the people, realize social progress and justice; fourth, firmly ensure national defense and security, social order, and safety; fifth, implement an independent, self-reliant, peaceful, friendly, cooperative, and development-oriented foreign policy; proactively and actively integrate internationally; sixth, build socialist democracy, implement great national unity, strengthen and broaden the national united front; seventh, build a socialist law-governed state of the people, by the people, and for the people; eighth, build the Party to be clean and strong” (Communist Party of Vietnam, 2011, p. 72).
These orientations are closely related, mutually reinforcing, and contribute together to the realization of the goals of socialism.

Reviewing 35 years of renovation, the country has attained major, historically significant achievements on the path of socialist construction and defending the socialist Fatherland.

3.
Conclusion
It can be seen that the 13th National Party Congress both demonstrated continuity and introduced many new and developed contents appropriate to the new situation and requirements of the cause of building socialism in Vietnam in the current period.
Notably, the view that “the people are the root” stands out, with persistent implementation of the principle “the people know, the people discuss, the people do, the people inspect, the people supervise, the people benefit.” The recognition and expression of the people as both the subject and the center of the renovation process have become increasingly profound and comprehensive, embodying the humane and humanistic nature of the renovation cause oriented towards socialism in Vietnam.
However, with a practical and objectively dialectical perspective, our Party is also fully aware that the aspiration to build a prosperous nation and ensure the people's happiness is by no means a simple or easy undertaking; thus, we must not be subjective, deluded, or impatient.
Especially in 2020 (and potentially for a long period thereafter), the Covid-19 pandemic had a profound impact on our country, causing considerable socio-economic damage and affecting national development.
Therefore, the 13th National Party Congress anticipated: “The global and domestic situation presents opportunities and advantages as well as intertwined difficulties and challenges, posing many new, heavier, and more complex issues and requirements for the cause of national construction and defense” (Communist Party of Vietnam, vol.
1, p. 109).
The Congress placed greater emphasis on domestic risks that require attention and effective remedial solutions.
